**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines acceptable and prohibited uses of MedCaribe Health Group technology, systems, and data. It establishes behavioural expectations for all individuals who access MedCaribe resources, with the goal of protecting patient data, reducing security risk, and meeting obligations under the Trinidad and Tobago Data Protection Act (2011).

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all MedCaribe employees, contractors, temporary staff, and any third party granted access to MedCaribe systems, networks, or data - regardless of location, device ownership, or employment status.

Systems covered include but are not limited to: Microsoft 365 (email, Teams, OneDrive, SharePoint), CloudMed EHR, QuickBooks Online, insurance portals, workstations, laptops, tablets, printers, network infrastructure, and internet access provided by MedCaribe.

---

## 3. General Principles

3.1 All MedCaribe technology and data are organizational assets. Access is granted to support job functions and may be monitored, audited, or revoked at any time.

3.2 Users are personally responsible for the security of their accounts, devices, and any data they access or handle.

3.3 Patient data and other sensitive information must be handled in accordance with the Data Classification Policy (POL-004) and the Data Protection Act.

3.4 When in doubt about whether an action is permitted, staff must consult the Operations Manager or IT Administrator before proceeding.

---

## 4. Acceptable Use

4.1 **Email and Communications:** Use MedCaribe-issued Microsoft 365 email for all business communication. Microsoft Teams is the approved platform for internal messaging and collaboration. Communicate with patients only through approved clinical systems or official MedCaribe channels.

4.2 **Internet Access:** Internet access is provided for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted during breaks provided it does not interfere with work, consume excessive bandwidth, or involve prohibited activities.

4.3 **File Storage:** Store business documents in OneDrive or SharePoint. Clinical records must be entered and maintained in CloudMed EHR. Financial records must be maintained in QuickBooks Online.

4.4 **Mobile Devices:** Staff using personal mobile devices for MedCaribe work (such as M365 mobile apps) must maintain a screen lock and keep the device operating system up to date.

4.5 **Passwords:** Use a unique, strong password for each MedCaribe system. Do not reuse passwords across personal and work accounts. Do not share passwords with anyone, including colleagues and IT support.

---

## 5. Prohibited Use

The following actions are strictly prohibited:

5.1 **Patient data on personal platforms:** Sending, receiving, or storing patient information via personal email (Gmail, Yahoo, Hotmail), personal messaging applications (WhatsApp, Telegram, Signal), personal cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox), or any platform not explicitly approved by MedCaribe for that purpose.

5.2 **Email forwarding:** Automatically forwarding MedCaribe email to personal or external email accounts.

5.3 **Credential sharing:** Sharing login credentials for any MedCaribe system with any person, including colleagues. This includes writing passwords on paper, sticky notes, or shared documents.

5.4 **Unauthorized software:** Installing software, browser extensions, or applications on MedCaribe workstations without prior approval from the IT Administrator.

5.5 **Removable media:** Using personal USB drives to transfer business data between devices or locations without encryption and prior approval. Approved encrypted USB devices will be provided where needed.

5.6 **Bypassing security controls:** Disabling antivirus software, ignoring update prompts for more than 48 hours, connecting to unauthorized Wi-Fi networks from MedCaribe devices, or taking any action intended to circumvent security measures.

5.7 **Illegal or harmful activity:** Using MedCaribe systems for any activity that is illegal under Trinidad and Tobago law, that could damage MedCaribe's reputation, or that constitutes harassment, discrimination, or threats.

5.8 **Unauthorized access:** Accessing systems, data, or patient records that are not required for your job function. Attempting to access another user's account, email, or files without explicit authorization.

---

## 6. Monitoring and Privacy

6.1 MedCaribe reserves the right to monitor, log, and audit all use of organizational systems including email, internet activity, file access, and login activity.

6.2 Users should have no expectation of privacy when using MedCaribe systems or accessing MedCaribe data, even from personal devices.

6.3 Monitoring is conducted for security, compliance, and operational purposes and is not used for general employee surveillance.

---

## 7. Reporting Obligations

7.1 All staff must immediately report any suspected security incident, policy violation, or suspicious activity to the Operations Manager or IT Administrator. This includes phishing emails, unusual account activity, lost or stolen devices, or unauthorized access to data.

7.2 Reports can be made verbally, via email to security@medcaribe.tt, or through the designated incident reporting process.

7.3 No staff member will face retaliation for reporting a suspected security incident or policy violation in good faith.

---

## 8. Enforcement

8.1 Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or contract.

8.2 Violations that result in a breach of the Data Protection Act may also be referred to the Office of the Information Commissioner and may carry legal penalties.

8.3 Enforcement decisions will be made by the Operations Manager in consultation with the Medical Director, with input from HR as appropriate.

---

## 9. Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy must be requested in writing to the Operations Manager and approved by the Medical Director. Approved exceptions will be documented, time-limited, and reviewed at each policy review cycle.
